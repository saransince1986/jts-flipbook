Progress

This unpiloted Russian resupply ship launched atop a Soyuz rocket brings 2.5 tons of fuel, equipment and other supplies to the International Space Station.

Shuttle

Although no longer in use, the American Space Shuttles were essential to the construction of the International Space Station, delivering many of the modules, crew and supplies.

Soyuz

A Russian Soyuz space capsule brought the first crew to the ISS in November 2000. Soyuz capsules continue to transport crews to and from the Station.

Returning to Earth

Soyuz space capsules use parachutes to land on the
flat steppe of Kazakhstan
in Central Asia. The return
to Earth takes less than
3½ hours.

Automatic Transfer Vehicle

The European Automated Transfer Vehicle launches on an Ariane 5 rocket to bring more than 16,900 pounds of cargo to the International Space Station.

Dragon

Launched atop a Falcon 9 rocket, the SpaceX Dragon was the first commercial spacecraft to deliver cargo to the International Space Station.

Cygnus

The Cygnus commercial spacecraft, developed by Orbital Sciences, launches atop an Antares rocket to bring suppllies to the International Space Station.

H-II Transfer Vehicle

Designed, built and launched in Japan, the H-II Transfer Vehicle delivers 12,000 pounds of cargo to the International Space Station.
