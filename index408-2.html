Eating in space

Swipe to begin

A full table

Eating is very different in space. But one thing is the same: people like to do it together.

Space table manners

The dining area is an important social space. But if there’s no room at your spacecraft’s table, it’s OK to eat on the ceiling.

Dinner adrift

Velcro keeps cans and pouches of food from floating off the table. Astronauts stay put using toeholds under the table.

The sauce secret

Many space meals contain thick, sticky sauce. It keeps food from drifting apart. Plus, who doesn’t like sauce?

Just like Mom’s

In space, Thanksgiving dinner comes in pouches. There’s only one thing missing . . .

Potatoes without gravy?!

A space Thanksgiving has no gravy. Goopy gravy is hard to apply to food in space, and pre-mixing potatoes and gravy ruins their texture!

More than nutritious

Even though it comes out of a pouch, space food has to look, feel, and taste good. This helps keep astronauts in high spirits.

Eating healthy

Astronauts need to eat a healthy, balanced diet. Weightlessness is hard on the body, and astronauts have a big workload.

Planning ahead

Imagine a mission to space lasting several years. You could pack a lot of food for the trip, but there may be other ways to keep yourself fed.

Growing dinner

Astronauts are experimenting with growing vegetables on the International Space Station. One day it might be possible to feed astronauts from a spacecraft garden.

Future food

We’ll need food wherever the journey to space takes us. Each destination will present new challenges to food production. This artist imagines underground greenhouses on frigid Mars.
