During the early years of space flight, astronauts taped fecal containment bags to their bodies when they had to go. A special “finger tube” would let the astronaut dislodge any bits of waste without touching it. Once sealed, the bag was stored for the duration of the mission.

It was common on Apollo flights for the astronaut using the fecal containment system to strip naked in an effort to avoid getting feces all over his clothes. From start to clean up, the whole thing could take up to an hour to complete.

In space today, urine is recycled into water for the astronauts to drink. Solid waste is passed by astronauts into small bags which get stored in a large can for about a week before they are sealed and jettisoned with trash that’s left to burn up in the atmosphere.
