Sleeping in space

Swipe to begin

Get rest, stay alert

Sleep is important. You don’t want a drowsy, irritable astronaut at the controls of a spacecraft.

Burning both ends

Like many of us, astronauts don’t get quite as much sleep as they should. Work, excitement, and motion sickness can cut hours from their scheduled eight hours
of sleep.

16 days a day

Even though astronauts on the International Space Station spend most of their time in artificial light, the 90-minute day/night cycle makes sleeping tough.

Downtime

While off-duty, your sleep station is a place where you can get some time alone. This astronaut is doing some sewing.

A little privacy

This cosmonaut is using his computer in his sleep station. Your station makes a cramped workspace, but at least no one can read over your shoulder.

Curl up and fly

In space, a sleeping body doesn’t lie flat. Astronauts strap themselves down before bed, and let their arms curl comfortably as they rest.

Artificial gravity?

By spinning part of a spacecraft like a carnival ride, engineers could create an area of artificial gravity. Astronauts could sleep and exercise better on the spinning “walls.”

A spinning city

Artists imagine space colonies that constantly spin to create the effect of gravity. You wouldn’t float in this colony—working and sleeping would feel more normal.

Sleeping any which way

Three astronauts tuck themselves into their sleep stations. With no real “up” or “down” in space, sleeping on the ceiling is just fine.

The science of sleep

Researchers study astronauts’ sleep patterns. Understanding the challenges of sleeping in space will help us stay healthy on long-duration missions.

Sleeping through stress

A long mission—like a multi-year trip to Mars—would be stressful. Hard work. Monotony. Danger. Tight quarters. How would you sleep?

Commander Van Winkle

Scientists are studying whether astronauts could sleep through long voyages. Putting a crew into hibernation for months at a time would save energy, food, and water. And it sounds relaxing.
